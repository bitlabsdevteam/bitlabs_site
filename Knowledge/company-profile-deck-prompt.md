# BitLabs Company Profile Deck Prompt

Use this prompt to generate a persuasive, enterprise-appropriate company profile deck for BitLabs.

## Master Prompt

```text
You are a senior B2B brand strategist, enterprise AI solutions consultant, and top-tier presentation copywriter.

Create a polished company profile deck for BitLabs that is designed to persuade potential customers and leave a strong, credible impression. The deck should help enterprise buyers quickly understand what BitLabs does, why it is different, and why it is a trustworthy AI delivery partner.

The output must be bilingual:
- English
- Japanese

Generate a 10-12 slide deck for this audience:
- CTOs
- CIOs
- Heads of AI
- Innovation leaders
- Product leaders
- Operations leaders
- Enterprise decision-makers evaluating an AI partner

The tone must be:
- executive-credible
- clear
- factual
- confident
- technical but understandable
- persuasive without hype
- premium and modern

The deck must feel suitable for a first serious customer meeting. It should show technical depth, research credibility, enterprise readiness, and delivery discipline.

BitLabs facts and positioning to use:
- BitLabs is a Tokyo-based AI R&D and consulting company.
- BitLabs helps organizations move from AI experimentation to secure, governed, production-grade systems.
- BitLabs combines research depth with production discipline.
- BitLabs focuses on practical AI systems that work under real business constraints.
- BitLabs designs, builds, evaluates, and deploys enterprise AI systems end to end.

Core capability areas to cover:
- AI agent solutions
- Enterprise AI architecture and delivery
- LLM pre-training and fine-tuning
- SLM design and training from scratch
- Custom React and AI application development
- Prompt engineering, context engineering, and evaluation harness design
- Secure cloud deployment for LLM systems
- OpenClaw custom solution implementation
- Ongoing AI research

Key strategic themes to emphasize:
- practical AI, not hype
- research depth translated into real delivery
- security by design
- evaluation and reliability as core system components
- governance-aware implementation
- controlled deployment and operational readiness
- measurable business value
- enterprise-grade delivery under real constraints

Differentiate BitLabs using these ideas:
- more than prototypes
- capable of moving AI into controlled production use
- strong in architecture, evaluation, and operational rigor
- suited for organizations with security, governance, and reliability requirements
- able to combine model work, application delivery, and deployment planning in one team

Do not make claims that are not verified. Strictly avoid:
- naming confidential clients
- inventing customer stories or case studies
- claiming certifications unless explicitly provided
- promising guaranteed ROI
- claiming zero hallucinations, perfect safety, or error-free outputs
- exaggerated phrases like "revolutionary", "world-leading", "magical", or "guaranteed transformation"
- empty buzzword stacking

If a strong proof point is needed but not provided, use safe positioning language instead of fabricating evidence.

Required deck structure:
1. Cover slide: positioning headline and short subtitle
2. Market problem / buyer pain: why enterprise AI efforts stall
3. What BitLabs does: concise company overview
4. Core services: service categories and what each enables
5. Research and technical depth: why BitLabs has technical credibility
6. Delivery model: how BitLabs moves from discovery to operations
7. Security and deployment posture: how BitLabs approaches risk, governance, and controlled deployment
8. Why BitLabs: differentiation and reasons to trust the team
9. Engagement fit: what kinds of customers and projects are a strong fit
10. Call to action: how to start a conversation with BitLabs

You may add 1-2 optional slides only if they increase persuasive quality:
- company facts / corporate profile
- capability matrix
- founder / leadership profile

For each slide, provide:
- Slide number
- Slide title
- Slide objective
- Key message
- English slide copy
- Japanese slide copy
- Suggested visual direction

Writing rules:
- Use concise, presentation-ready language
- Prefer strong headlines and tight supporting bullets
- Make each slide feel purposeful and easy to scan
- Keep body copy dense enough to show substance, but not overloaded
- Maintain a clear narrative progression from problem to credibility to action
- Make the Japanese natural and business-appropriate, not literal machine translation
- Avoid repeating the same sentence structure across slides

Design direction for the deck:
- modern enterprise presentation
- minimal but premium
- editorial layout
- strong typography hierarchy
- intentional whitespace
- subtle confidence, not flashy startup energy
- visual cues of AI systems, architecture, reliability, and secure deployment

Where useful, include phrasing that signals:
- research depth and production discipline
- governed AI systems
- operational reliability
- deployment readiness
- technical rigor
- enterprise trust

Use these business-safe claims:
- BitLabs focuses on practical, secure, production-oriented AI systems.
- BitLabs can support enterprise AI design, implementation, evaluation, and deployment.
- BitLabs emphasizes measurable outcomes, system control, and operational discipline.
- BitLabs is positioned for organizations that need more than a prototype.

Company details available for optional corporate profile content:
- Company name: Bit Labs株式会社
- CEO: David Bong
- Established: April 3, 2024
- Location: Tokyo, Japan

End the output with:
1. a recommended slide order
2. a one-paragraph presenter narrative for the full deck
3. 3 alternate cover-slide headline options in both English and Japanese

Output format:
- Use clear section dividers for each slide
- Keep the structure consistent across all slides
- Do not output speaker notes unless they are very short and highly useful
- Do not output generic advice before the deck

Now generate the full bilingual company profile deck.
```

## Suggested Usage

- Use as-is for a first-pass company profile deck.
- Add approved proof points later if you want a stronger sales version.
- If you need a shorter deck, change `10-12 slide deck` to `6-8 slide deck`.
- If you need a Japan-first version, move Japanese above English in the output instructions.
