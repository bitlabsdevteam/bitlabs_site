## Research Areas

BitLabs is not only an implementation company. It also has an active research orientation focused on where modern AI systems fail, drift, or become difficult to trust in production.

### Main Research Themes

#### SLM Architecture and Training Efficiency

BitLabs studies small language model architecture design and training efficiency for constrained deployment environments.

This is relevant when:

- Cost matters
- Latency matters
- On-device or tightly controlled deployment matters
- Smaller deployable systems are more practical than very large models

#### Fine-tuning and Domain Adaptation

BitLabs explores methods for adapting model behavior to specific domains and requirements.

This includes concerns such as:

- Relevance
- Accuracy
- Steerability
- Alignment to operational use cases

#### Bias Detection and Mitigation

BitLabs has active interest in detecting implicit bias in LLM outputs and exploring mitigation methods, including approaches such as FairSteer and hook-based interventions.

Important framing:

- The goal is not to claim bias can be eliminated completely
- The goal is to make bias more detectable, measurable, and reducible through disciplined methods

#### Agentic System Design

BitLabs researches orchestration, memory design, planning behavior, and reliability boundaries for agentic systems.

This includes questions such as:

- When should an agent act versus ask for confirmation?
- How should memory be structured?
- How should tools be gated?
- What failure modes appear in long-running workflows?
- How should evaluations measure agent quality?

#### Evaluation, Robustness, and Governance

BitLabs studies how to evaluate production-grade model systems across quality, latency, safety, and operational reliability.

This is a central theme, not an afterthought.

## Reliability and Evaluation Stance

BitLabs treats evaluation and guardrails as core system components.

Key principles:

- Quality must be measured, not assumed
- Latency matters alongside output quality
- Safety and failure modes must be tested explicitly
- Tool use and planning behavior need evaluation, not just prompting
- Release decisions should align with governance requirements

### Reliability Practices

- Structured evaluation suites
- Testing across quality, latency, and safety dimensions
- Reliability analysis for tool use and planning behavior
- Review of operational failure modes
- Release criteria before production deployment

### Important Non-Claim

BitLabs should not be described as guaranteeing zero hallucinations, perfect safety, or fully error-free outputs. The correct framing is disciplined evaluation, bounded risk, and operational reliability improvement.
