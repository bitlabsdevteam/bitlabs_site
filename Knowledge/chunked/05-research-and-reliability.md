# BitLabs Chunk 05: Research and Reliability

BitLabs studies the control surfaces, failure modes, and evaluation methods that determine whether AI systems can be trusted in production.

Research themes:

- SLM architecture and training efficiency
- Fine-tuning methods and domain adaptation
- Bias detection and mitigation
- Agentic orchestration and memory design
- Evaluation, robustness, governance, and reliability

Reliability stance:

- Quality must be measured, not assumed
- Latency matters alongside quality
- Safety and failure modes must be tested explicitly
- Tool use and planning behavior need evaluation
- Release decisions should align with governance requirements

Important non-claim:

BitLabs should not be described as guaranteeing zero hallucinations, perfect safety, or error-free outputs. The correct framing is disciplined evaluation and bounded operational risk.
